The following document is a style guide to assist in the research and designing of U-LEAF application. This document outlines the overall file structure, naming conventions, design and coding guidelines, styles and resources to be used in the designing of the pages. To ensure design continuity and maintain the lifespan of application, it is important that everybody involved in the research, design maintenance and updating of sites read, understand and adhere to this guide.

Branding elements should always remain consistent. They exist in order to reinforce the U-LEAF clients identity and keep visual unity. Some of these elements also serve as navigating tools.

Interactions: Clicking on the U-LEAF / U-LEAF Clients logo takes a user back to the home page (Incident List) of U-LEAF at any time.

1. Logo U-LEAF

It is vitally important to use U-LEAF / U-LEAF Client logo correctly and consistently at all times. Do not change the colours, distort or alter the logo in any way.

It is important that U-LEAF logo be readable at all times, so be sure adequate clear space surrounds the U-LEAF logo. No elements should encroach on or crowd out the logo. The preferred placement of U-LEAF logo is in the upper left-hand side of an application. Please refer to the Logo Placement section of the toolkit for more information.

2. Protected area space

To preserve the Unisys / U-LEAF client logo's integrity, always maintain a minimum clear space around the logo. This clear space isolates the logo from competing graphic elements such as other logos, copy,photography or background patterns that may divert attention. 3. Logo Size

U-LEAF logo size is 83px x 19px. It is not allowed to alter the size of the U-LEAF logo. U-LEAF clients logo should not exceed the width of 115px and height of 40px at any time.

4. colour

Colour plays a powerful role in U-LEAF visual system. The U-LEAF colour palette employs two colours - including U-LEAF Dark Blue-which support the bold, innovative thinking and corporate we deliver to our clients through U-LEAF and Dark Green which support BVJ installations.

Because the U-LEAF colour palette features bold colours, consider using fewer versus more colours for maximum impact and a sophisticated visual style. Here are some tips for consideration:

  • Use only one colour for all headlines and subheads on inside spreads against a white background.
  • To ensure contrast when using colour typography over colour blocks, consider using tints for greater text legibility and to support messaging hierarchy.
  • Restrict the use of primary colour text over primary colour blocks to headlines or blurbs for maximum impact and legibility—especially on screen to avoid "vibrating text."
